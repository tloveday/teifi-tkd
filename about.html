What is Taekwondo?

Taekwondo is a Martial Art from Korea, characterised by punching and kicking techniques with a focus on fast kicking techniques. It’s name is composed of three parts as shown in the English spelling, though it is one word in Korean. "Tae" means "foot,"; "Kwon" means "fist,"; and "Do" means the "way". It is quite literally the Way of the Foot and the Fist.

There are two main styles of Taekwondo.

  • World Taekwondo (WT) formally World Taekwondo Federation (WTF) is commonly known as 'Olympic Taekwondo'. It is the style seen in the Olympics since its inclusion at Sydney 2000. Wales’ own Jade Jones is a Double Olympic Champion claiming the tites in London 2012 and Rio De janerio in 2016.
  • International Taekwondo Federation (ITF) the style in which we at Teifi Taekwondo train. Although not the Olympic style, it doesn’t mean that you cannot one day become Olympic Champion- Ms Jones herself started in the ITF style before changing to the WT rules set.

Frequently Asked Questions

Our classes take place in a matted Dojang. Takewondo is done with bare feet so there's no need to buy expensive trainers.We suggest that all new starters wear loose fitting clothing (without zips or buttons) until you have obtained your Dobok (provided with your membership).

Additional items can be purchased from the Coaches. We also suggest that students come with a bottle of water and a if preferred a towel.

All students require a licence and insurance. We have placed these in the annual membership to Teifi Taekwondo.

We have found that if parents are not present its very difficult for them to understand what the child has to learn to achieve the next belt, it also makes the getting sparring equipment on and off a little easier.

Sparring equipment can be purchased at any point in during your training journey, however All students will need sparring equipment when they are promoted to Green Belt Blue Stripe.

Your sparring equipment consists of head guard, hand pads, feet pads, shin pads, gum shield, and either a groin guard (Boys & Men) or breast protectors (ladies). Due to the volume of equipment a bag to hold them in is suggested.

Grading for belts is purely optional. The belt system sets goals for students and encourages them to learn new things and face fresh challenges. However, if a student does not wish to grade, we will still be happy for them to train with us.

There are 4 coloured belt gradings a year. Every March, June, September and December.

There are 2 black belt gradings a year. Every April and October
