What is Taekwondo?

Taekwondo is a Martial Art from Korea, characterised by punching and kicking techniques with a focus on fast kicking techniques. It’s name is composed of three parts as shown in the English spelling, though it is one word in Korean. "Tae" means "foot,"; "Kwon" means "fist,"; and "Do" means the "way". It is quite literally the Way of the Foot and the Fist.

There are two main styles of Taekwondo.

  • World Taekwondo (WT) formally World Taekwondo Federation (WTF) is commonly known as 'Olympic Taekwondo'. It is the style seen in the Olympics since its inclusion at Sydney 2000. Wales’ own Jade Jones is a Double Olympic Champion claiming the tites in London 2012 and Rio De janerio in 2016.
  • International Taekwondo Federation (ITF) the style in which we at Teifi Taekwondo train. Although not the Olympic style, it doesn’t mean that you cannot one day become Olympic Champion- Ms Jones herself started in the ITF style before changing to the WT rules set.
